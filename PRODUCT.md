# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Recruvault serves two professional audiences in Australian cleared and government-adjacent hiring. Recruiters manage open roles, move candidates through review, shortlist, interview, offer, and placement, and maintain an auditable record of sensitive access. Job seekers manage applications, resumes, cover letters, reusable credentials, and the information they share with each organisation.

Both groups use the product during focused, consequential work. Recruiters need to understand their hiring pipeline and next actions at a glance. Job seekers need one reliable record of every application and the career documents used for it, without losing control of sensitive information.

## Product Purpose

Recruvault is a hiring workspace that helps recruiters place job seekers into roles and helps job seekers manage applications and reusable career documents. It replaces fragmented spreadsheets, inbox threads, and repeated uploads with a clear role-to-placement pipeline supported by secure, expiring, audited exchange for resumes, cover letters, and clearance details. Identity documents, right-to-work evidence, and police-check documents are deliberately out of scope.

Success means recruiters know which candidates need action and can progress them confidently toward placement. Job seekers can see where every application stands, keep current versions of their resume and cover letters, and reuse information without surrendering control.

Both audiences can connect trusted tools through a role-aware REST API or the included MCP server. Organisation keys inherit recruiter permissions; job-seeker keys are restricted to the owner's application and career-record data.

A fresh installation starts with a one-time recruiter setup. The first user creates the verified owner account and organisation together; setup closes as soon as that organisation exists.

## Brand Personality

Assured, exacting, and discreet. The product should feel like a mature professional workspace: calm enough for long sessions, dense enough for serious work, and human without becoming casual. It earns trust through clear records, predictable behaviour, and precise language rather than decorative security theatre.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-generated SaaS dashboards, cool corporate navy palettes, purple gradients, glass effects, oversized metric tiles, repeated icon-card grids, and ornamental illustrations. Avoid consumer job-board styling, gamified progress, dark operations-console aesthetics, applicant-tracking systems that reduce people to opaque records, and security interfaces that manufacture drama. The interface should never feel like a landing page inside an authenticated workflow.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with placement progress. Show roles, candidates, pipeline stage, deadlines, and next actions before explanation or decoration.
2. Give job seekers one career record. Applications, resumes, cover letters, credentials, and sharing history should feel like one coherent workspace.
3. Make trust inspectable. Surface consent, expiry, audit, retention, and document state in plain language at the point they matter.
4. Design for scanning. Use stable hierarchy, compact rows, aligned figures, and selective charts so a busy professional can orient in seconds.
5. Preserve user agency. Make status, sharing, revocation, deletion, and access boundaries explicit and reversible wherever the underlying action allows it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain full keyboard operation, visible focus, semantic landmarks and headings, robust labels, sufficient contrast, and status cues that never rely on colour alone. Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep interaction motion brief and functional, provide useful small-screen layouts, and write in plain Australian English for users with different levels of security and hiring expertise.
